Publications

I began my academic research in my second semester of my graduate program at Fordham. Pivoting from my business degree, I wanted to dig into some high-impact and rapidly evolving topics to complement my studies in fundamental data science techniques. My research spans topics like game theory, federated learning, quantum, and robotics.

Explore my publications below, and find related slide decks here:

Efficient Data Encoding in Quantum Neural Networks (2025)

This paper introduces Adaptive Threshold Pruning (ATP), an encoding method that improves the efficiency and scalability of Quantum Neural Networks (QNNs). ATP dynamically prunes non-essential data features using adaptive thresholds, reducing entanglement and circuit complexity without sacrificing model performance. ATP is shown to lower entanglement entropy and enhance adversarial robustness, especially when combined with methods like FGSM.

Resilience for Multi-Layer Heterogeneous Robotic Networks (2025)

This paper introduces a framework for maintaining continuous connectivity among heterogeneous agents. Unlike traditional approaches focused on homogeneous swarms, this work models multi-layered systems with varying security and connectivity requirements. We propose a (k₁, k₂)-connectivity metric that extends standard k-connectivity to capture diverse resilience needs and ensure robust communication even under link failures.

Securing Federated Learning for Autonomous Driving (2024)

This paper presents SecFedDrive, a defense framework for federated learning (FL) models in autonomous driving systems. While FL protects data privacy by enabling collaborative training without data sharing, it remains vulnerable to backdoor attacks that can compromise model integrity and passenger safety. SecFedDrive introduces a robust, decentralized defense mechanism that mitigates such attacks, reducing their success rate to as low as 2%.

Cost Poisoning Attacks in Linear-Quadratic Zero-Sum Differential Games (2023)

This paper investigates policy poisoning attacks in learning-based control systems through the lens of linear-quadratic (LQ) zero-sum differential games. We model an adversary that manipulates cost measurements in batch data to mislead agents during policy learning, exposing vulnerabilities to cost-poisoning attacks. Through case studies, we demonstrate how such attacks can compromise system performance and reliability.

Equitable Power Resilience in NYC (2023)

This journal paper addresses power resilience inequity in New York City by strategically allocating infrastructure to strengthen the grid during high demand and disasters. We develop a power resilience inequity metric to identify the most affected communities and analyze relationships among resilience, charging access, and socioeconomic factors. Using data-driven modeling, we predict how targeted resource deployment can reduce inequities. See here for a shorter conference publication.
